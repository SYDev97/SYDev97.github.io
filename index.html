Project Proposal: Rental Property Managemet & Listing Platform

Brief Overview

The proposed project will be a rental property management platform & company. The idea behind the company & platform would be to give homeowners to tools & marketplace to rent out their properties over a longer period. The project would not only be designed as a website but would also be designed to assist property owners throughout the entire process and even give the user access to dedicated teams of professionals to assist them.

How would this benefit people?

In 2016, the Australian census shows that the percentage of Australians renting had increased to 30.9% (1) of the population and homeowners make up around 60% (2) of the market.

Developing such a platform would enable homeowners who have little to no experience renting out their properties to gain access to a second stream of income by using such a platform. Not only this, but having a such a platform widely available to the general public, it would allow these home owners to rent out their properties and spare rooms without having to pay up to 15% of their weekly rent (3) on management costs.

Detailed Description

The property management platform would be designed in a way that would allow everyday homeowners who have no experience renting or managing a property to do so. The platform would be designed to allow property owners to rent out their homes, apartment or spare rooms over longer periods of times, rather than by a single nightly rate like other similar platform. The platform itself as well as the company behind the platform would contain several different sections and features to make this easier.

As part of this platform, it would be designed to contain multiple section, a public property listing, a "renters" dashboard and a "managers" dashboard. With each section playing a role in how the entire platform operates different.

The first section, the public property listing, would be a place where any user (account or not) could browse through the properties available for rent on the platform. It would also indicate if the property was currently rented and when the property would become vacant again. As part of a safety and security measure, the platform would automatically hide the address of the property from the public listing when the property is being rented out.

The second section, would be the renters dashboard. As part of the renters dashboard, they would be given access to tools that would allow them to easily contact the landlord, manage a current rental, apply for another rental & even manage payments for their current rental. The dashboard for the renter would be designed in a way that allows them easy control and access to information about the property and the landlord as well as information and the remaining duration of their lease.

The final section of the platform would be the manager’s dashboard. As part of the manager dashboard, the user would be given access to tools that would allow them to book inspections of their property to ensure the tenant is keeping it clean and tidy, manage applications for new renters, manage the current tenant, get in contact with current and future tenants of the property and more. The manage dashboard would also allow the user to manage multiple properties as well as manage the information that is on the website about their properties. This information would include images of the property, description, weekly or monthly rent as well as giving the user an option to mark their property as ready to be rented out or not.

Tools, Technolofy & Skills

Ideally this platform would be designed and developed using a Software as a Service model, meaning that the website would designed and developed as a web based. To have this completed successfully, the development team would require a basic knowledge of the MERN technology stack or some equivalent.

The MERN technology stack is a JavaScript stack cantered around using MongoDB, the ExpressJS framework, React JS and Node.js as its core libraries. To ensure this style of software can run smoothly, the platform would have to hosted on a dedicated server, a Virtual Private Server or some equivalent of a cloud hosting that supports NodeJS applications.

All other libraries and frameworks that would be required for such a platform, including; session management, cookies, payment processing are already open source.

Overall Outcome

If this project were to be successful, it would not only disrupt the rental market in Australia, it could also be expanded upon to become an international platform and help disrupt the property rental market in other countries. Not only this, but if this platform were successful it would give property owners access to a passive income from their properties or spare rooms at home.
